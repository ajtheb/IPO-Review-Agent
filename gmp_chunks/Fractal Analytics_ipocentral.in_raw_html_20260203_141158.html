Fractal Analytics IPO GMP, Price, Date, Allotment

By
Mahesh Yadav
-
December 30, 2025
1

Fractal Analytics IPO Description – Founded in 2000, Fractal Analytics is a globally recognized enterprise artificial intelligence (AI) company that empowers human decision-making through AI-driven insights and solutions. As of 31 March 2025, the company operates under two key segments—Fractal.ai, which delivers AI services and products primarily hosted on its flagship platform Cogentiq, and Fractal Alpha, which manages independent AI businesses.

Fractal serves 113 “Must Win Clients” (MWCs), including global enterprises such as Citi, Nestlé, Mars, and Philips, with its top ten clients contributing 53.8% of Fractal.ai’s revenue in Fiscal 2025. The firm’s expertise spans consumer packaged goods and retail (CPGR), technology, media and telecom (TMT), healthcare and life sciences (HLS), and banking, financial services, and insurance (BFSI).

Recognized by Everest Group and Forrester as a leader in AI and analytics, Fractal has developed proprietary foundation models such as Kalaido.ai, Vaidya.ai, and Fathom-R1-14B, alongside pioneering initiatives like Project Ramanujan. With over 24 patents and 41 applications, Fractal emphasizes research, innovation, and client-centricity, achieving an NPS of 77 and maintaining a “Great Place to Work” certification across seven countries.

Promoters of Fractal Analytics – Srikanth Velamakanni, Pranay Agrawal, Chetana Kumar, Narendra Kumar Agrawal, and Rupa Krishnan Agrawal

Fractal Analytics Offer Objectives

The company proposes to utilize the Net Proceeds from the Issue towards the following objects:

  • Investment in one of its subsidiaries, Fractal USA, for pre-payment and/ or scheduled repayment, in full or in part, of its borrowings – INR 264.9 crore
  • Purchase of laptops – INR 57.1 crore
  • Setting up new office premises in India – INR 121.1 crore
  • Investment in (a) research and development, and (b) sales and marketing under Fractal Alpha – INR 355.1 crore
  • Funding inorganic growth through unidentified acquisitions and other strategic initiatives, and general corporate purposes
